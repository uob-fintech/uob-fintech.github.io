Overview

About the collaboration

This page documents the inaugural University of Bristol–IIT Bombay FinTech Research Collaboration, held at the University of Bristol on 1–2 July 2026. The two-day programme combined a FinTech Research Workshop with a Strategic Collaboration Roundtable, establishing shared research priorities and identifying opportunities for joint funding, researcher mobility and doctoral collaboration.

The collaboration builds on a series of UK–India strategic initiatives, including the UK–India AI Opportunities Dialogue, the University of Bristol–IIT Bombay Memorandum of Understanding, and support from the University of Bristol Faculty of Science and Engineering Global Positioning Fund.

The partnership also strengthens research-led education through the University of Bristol Mumbai Enterprise Campus and the MSc Financial Technology with Data Science, creating closer links between internationally collaborative research and postgraduate education.

Strategic Collaboration Roundtable

Venue: Goldney Hall Mahogany Parlour

Senior representatives from the University of Bristol and IIT Bombay met to develop a roadmap for long-term collaboration in FinTech research and education. Participants included institutional leaders, Principal Investigators and colleagues from both universities, with additional participation online from IIT Bombay leadership.

Discussion themes

  • UK–India collaborative funding opportunities
  • EPSRC and joint research grant applications
  • Strategic FinTech research priorities
  • Industry partnership and innovation funding
  • Staff and student mobility
  • Visiting scholar and academic exchange programmes
  • PhD co-supervision
  • Collaborative research and publications
  • Shared facilities and interdisciplinary opportunities
  • Future partnership roadmap and immediate actions

10 Nov
2025

UK–India AI Opportunities Dialogue

The collaboration builds on the UK–India AI Opportunities Dialogue, hosted by the Alan Turing Institute on behalf of the UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) and the British High Commission in India, and held at the Alan Turing Institute, British Library, London. Prof John Cartlidge and Dr Jin Zheng participated as invited experts representing the AI for FinTech and Inclusive Finance theme, helping establish relationships and research priorities that contributed to the development of the University of Bristol–IIT Bombay FinTech Research Collaboration.

The University of Bristol and IIT Bombay formalised their institutional partnership through a Memorandum of Understanding, providing the framework for long-term collaboration in research, education and academic exchange.

Looking ahead

The next phase of the collaboration

The University of Bristol and IIT Bombay are now progressing the next phase of their FinTech Research Collaboration. Building on the inaugural programme, the partnership will focus on four priorities that strengthen research, education and institutional engagement between both universities.

Collaborative research

Develop joint research proposals in Financial Technology, Artificial Intelligence and digital finance, with a focus on UK–India and international funding opportunities.

Researcher mobility

Expand opportunities for staff exchanges, visiting researchers and collaborative doctoral supervision to strengthen long-term academic partnerships.

Research-led education

Build stronger links between collaborative research and postgraduate education through the University of Bristol Mumbai Enterprise Campus and the MSc Financial Technology with Data Science.

Continuing the partnership

Establish a regular programme of workshops and research visits, beginning with the planned collaboration meeting at IIT Bombay in Spring 2027.
