Overview

A strategic partnership in FinTech

The University of Bristol and IIT Bombay FinTech Research Collaboration brings together researchers from both institutions to establish a long-term partnership in Financial Technology research and education. Combining complementary expertise in artificial intelligence for finance, machine learning, financial data science, blockchain and decentralised finance, multi-agent systems and agentic finance, and responsible financial innovation, the collaboration aims to develop internationally leading research while strengthening research-led education.

The partnership has developed through a series of strategic UK–India engagements, beginning with the UK–India AI Opportunities Dialogue convened under the Technology Security Initiative (TSI), followed by the University of Bristol–IIT Bombay Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed in early 2026. Supported by a major University of Bristol Faculty of Science and Engineering Global Positioning Fund award, these initiatives established the foundation for sustained collaboration between the two institutions.

The July 2026 Collaboration Programme represented one of the first major research activities delivered under this framework. Combining a public research workshop with a senior strategic roundtable, it provided a forum to exchange research ideas, identify shared priorities, develop future funding plans, and establish pathways for researcher mobility, doctoral collaboration, joint supervision and publications.

The initiative also supports the development of research-led education through the launch of the University of Bristol Mumbai Enterprise Campus and the MSc Financial Technology with Data Science. During the same week as the collaboration programme, newly appointed Mumbai Enterprise Campus academics participated in teaching development and curriculum planning in Bristol, reinforcing the close integration of collaborative research and education.

Building the partnership

From strategy to collaboration

10 Nov
2025

UK–India AI Opportunities Dialogue

Prof John Cartlidge and Dr Jin Zheng represented the FinTech and Inclusive Finance theme at an invitation-only Track 1.5 Dialogue hosted by The Alan Turing Institute on behalf of the UK Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office and the British High Commission in India.

Convened under the UK–India Technology Security Initiative, they were two of seven UK and Indian experts contributing to this theme, helping identify opportunities for bilateral AI and FinTech collaboration and proposed programmes for 2026–2027.

The two universities signed a Memorandum of Understanding, creating a formal foundation for broader institutional cooperation. The FinTech collaboration was developed as part of the work to build practical research and education activity under that agreement.

Related research: designing transnational STEM education

The Bristol FinTech team has produced a case-study paper on the design and planned delivery of the MSc Financial Technology with Data Science across Bristol and the Mumbai Enterprise Campus. The work proposes an integrated model combining block teaching, flying faculty and co-teaching to maintain academic consistency, coordinate interdisciplinary expertise and support embedded staff development in a new transnational education setting.

Paper: Designing Transnational STEM Education: A Case Study of the MSc Financial Technology with Data Science Across a UK–India Branch Campus (EDULEARN26).

Substantial investment in a long-term UK–India partnership

Supported by the University of Bristol Faculty of Science and Engineering Global Positioning Fund.

Building on the UK–India strategic engagement and the institutional Memorandum of Understanding, the award was secured by Prof John Cartlidge and Dr Jin Zheng, the project's Principal Investigators (PIs), who developed and led the initiative and organised the inaugural collaboration programme.

More than £20,000 of University of Bristol funding made the collaboration possible. It funded travel and participation for the IIT Bombay delegation and colleagues from the University of Bristol Mumbai Enterprise Campus, enabling them to come to Bristol for the research workshop, strategic roundtable, research exchange and teaching development.

The award supported the two-day programme at Goldney Hall, partnership planning, future joint funding development, researcher mobility discussions and preparation for research-led education at MEC. This was the principal enabling investment behind the collaboration.

The project was led through the School of Engineering Mathematics and Technology (SEMT) within the University of Bristol Faculty of Science and Engineering.
